Introduction

The greatest abundance of marine fish and the highest biodiversity are associated with coral reefs, which provide essential habitats for the fishes and their prey. Coral reef fishes are one of the most colourful and diverse groups of animals in the sea. Their life cycles are closely linked to the reef environment for feeding, shelter, reproduction, and growth.

While India has a vast coastline of over 8,000 km , its coral and rocky reef areas are less extensive compared to other regions of the world. These important ecosystems are distributed in specific locations along the east and west coasts , including major reef areas like the Gulf of Mannar, Lakshadweep, the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, and the Gulf of Kutch.

About This Guide

This digital database is based on the Field Guide on Reef Associated Fishes of India , a comprehensive guide for the quick identification of ornamental and commercial reef-associated fishes in Indian waters. It was authored by Dr. Rekha J. Nair and Dr. Somy Kuriakose and published in 2014 by the Central Marine Fisheries Research Institute (CMFRI) as CMFRI Special Publication No. 117.

The guide documents over 372 fish species from 66 families , compiled from numerous visits to harbors along India's southern coastline and from underwater surveys conducted at Lakshadweep and the Andaman islands. The project was funded by the Ministry of Environment and Forests. This work aims to support researchers, students, and sport divers by making the identification of reef fish easier, which is crucial for biological studies, trade monitoring, and conservation efforts.
